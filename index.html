Clause 0.1–1.2

What is IS 2502?

The Indian Standard governing precise bending, scheduling, and fixing of steel reinforcement bars used in concrete construction.

Clause 0.2

Bending Schedules

Assists designers, engineers, and contractors in drawing up precise bending schedules. Saves site time when rational shaping dimensions are supplied.

Clause 0.3

Skilled Practice

Bar bending requires adequate supervision, skilled workmanship, and efficient equipment. Unskilled labour will result in unsatisfactory work.

Clause 1.2

Scope

Applies to IS:432-1960 Mild Steel bars, with modifications to IS:1139 Deformed Bars and IS:1786 Cold Twisted Bars.

Clause 6.1–6.4

Bending Methods

Cold bending is primary. Hot bending only for bars >25mm at cherry-red heat, max 850°C.

Clause 6.1

Cold Bending

All bars bent cold unless otherwise provided. Any equipment with prior approval of engineer-in-charge.

PREFERRED ALL SIZES
Clause 6.1.1

Hot Bending

Bars >25mm may be bent at cherry-red heat, NOT exceeding 850°C, except cold-worked bars. Must NOT be quenched.

MAX 850°C >25mm
Clause 6.2

Equipment

Hand bender ≤16mm. Geared bender 16-35mm. Power-operated for 36mm+. Special rollers for deformed bars.

Clause 7.1–7.6

Fixing Reinforcement

Reinforcement must be maintained at designed positions throughout concreting.

1
Position Per Drawing
Clause 7.1.1

Reinforcement placed as per design drawing and secured. Position re-checked before concreting after any delay.

2
Lapping Bars
Clause 7.1.2

Lapping per IS:456-1964. Laps shall be staggered to avoid stress concentration.

3
Tying Bars
Clause 7.2

Crossing bars secured with annealed binding wire ≥0.90mm per IS:280. Compression bars tied in two perpendicular directions.

4
Cover Blocks
Clause 7.3

Cement mortar blocks ensure cover. Mortar not leaner than embedding concrete. Single block per section only.

5
Spacers
Clause 7.4

Maintain wall and slab reinforcement spacing using bent spacer bars or slotted spacer boards.

6
Column Ties
Clause 7.5

Column ties properly arranged in closed loops. Staggering allowed if pattern uniform in elevation.

Clause 8 & 9

Transport & Storage

Clause 8.1

Transport

Transport so reinforcement is not damaged or deformed. Separate bars by sizes and lengths at unloading.

Clause 9.1.1

Off-Ground

Bars NOT in direct contact with ground. Stack on timber sleepers or racks. Vertical stacking possible but harder to identify.

Clause 9.1.2

Labelling

Each bundle labelled with bar mark, schedule reference number, and laying position. Group small bars and stirrups.

Clause 9.1.4

Rust Control

Hard rust is desirable. Prevent loose scaling. Shelter the stack in corrosion-prone weather.

Key Terms

Glossary

Hook (H)
Length added for semicircular hook. H = 9d/11d/13d/17d (k=2/3/4/6). Min 75mm.
Bend (B)
Length added for right-angle bend. B = 5d/5.5d/6d/7d. Min 75mm.
Nominal Size (d)
Diameter of reinforcement bar in mm. Base unit for hook, bend, and radius calculations.
k Value
Internal bend radius factor (r = kd). 2 mild steel, 3 medium tensile, 4 cold twisted, 6 large bars >25mm.
Bending Schedule
Tabular document listing every bar's location, mark, size, number, shape, and dimensions.
Cover Block
Cement mortar block ensuring cover. Not leaner than embedding concrete. Single block per section only.
Cutting Length
Total bar length before bending = sum of dimensions + allowances, rounded up to next 25mm.
Mandrel
Pin around which bar is bent. Diameter = 2kd (= internal bend radius).
Stirrup
Closed loop binding main bars. Straight end beyond curve ≥ 8d.
Critical Radius
Radius above which bars need NOT be bent — curvature obtained during placing.
